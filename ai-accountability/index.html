The Defining Case Studies

What Buyers Are Trained to Avoid

NOT AI 1999 to 2025

Post Office Horizon

Fujitsu accounting software with hidden bugs. 700+ wrongful criminal convictions. The supplier concealed defects. The institution trusted outputs blindly. No independent audit rights.

AI lesson. Information asymmetry plus blind institutional trust equals catastrophic harm at scale.
AI SYSTEM Court ruling, Feb 2020

Dutch SyRI

An ML fraud detection algorithm secretly risk-scored low-income communities. The Hague District Court halted it, the first court ruling to stop an AI system on human rights grounds (ECHR Art. 8).

AI lesson. Hidden algorithmic harm to protected groups violates fundamental rights, and it will be litigated.
AI SYSTEM Disclosed in 2024

DWP Universal Credit AI

An ML model risk-scoring around 1M claims a year. Internal analysis showed bias by age, disability, marital status and nationality. Disclosed via FOI. Criticised for a "hurt first, fix later" approach.

AI lesson. Passive bias monitoring is not enough. Proactive disclosure is now expected.

These three cases are now standard references in procurement officer training. Buyers have been taught to look for exactly these patterns in supplier proposals.

The Compliance Picture

Six Instruments Shaping Every Tender

Procurement Act 2023

IN FORCE

New transparency obligations, contract management, exit requirements, and supplier exclusion provisions. Section 68 covers tech exit obligations. In force Feb 2025.

PPN 017 (Feb 2025)

MANDATORY

AI transparency in procurement, replacing PPN 02/24. Mandatory AI disclosure, transparency obligations, and evaluation questions for all central government and ALBs.

ATRS (CDDO, 2024)

MANDATORY

Algorithmic Transparency Recording Standard. Tier 1 public summary plus Tier 2 technical record. Must be accessible within 5 working days of request.

NHS DTAC (Apr 2026)

NHS ONLY

Updated form mandatory from 6 April 2026. Clinical safety (DCB0129/0160), data protection, technical security, and interoperability. A named clinical safety officer is required.

EU AI Act (Aug 2026)

IMMINENT

High-risk deadline arrives August 2026. Recruitment and workforce AI, plus public service delivery AI, sit in Annex III high-risk. Conformity assessments, human oversight, and bias testing are required.

PPN 002 Social Value Model

IN FORCE

Mandatory Adoption Criteria (MACs) for workforce development now intersect with AI displacement obligations. T3.1 reskilling and T1.2 employment creation are scored.

Risk Mitigation

Five credible dimensions buyers expect suppliers to evidence, not assert

Risk Mitigation

Five Dimensions of Credible AI Risk Governance

Transparency

AI Registry, ATRS Tier 2 record, named AI Governance Lead, board-level AI policy

Bias Assurance

Independent bias audit, all 9 protected characteristics, proactive disclosure, not reactive

Data Sovereignty

UK or EEA residency confirmed, US CLOUD Act legal opinion obtained, no undisclosed sub-processors

Exit Planning

Costed exit plan, open formats, transition support period, tested and documented

Continuous Monitoring

Live performance dashboard with buyer access, quarterly bias review, public incident reporting

The Horizon test. Could an independent auditor, with no access to your team, verify every claim you make about your AI system from documentation alone? If not, you have a governance gap.

The Must-Have Artefact

Your AI Risk Register

Not a theoretical exercise. A live document with named owners, reviewed quarterly, available on request within 5 working days.

Minimum Required Fields

01

AI system name, version, vendor, and functional description

02

Risk category, likelihood, impact (scored), inherent and residual

03

Named risk owner plus named technical owner, not just role titles

04

Mitigation actions, review cadence, last reviewed date

05

Bias and fairness assessment status plus independent audit flag

Common Buyer Red Flags

×

Register produced for this tender, not maintained operationally

×

Risks are theoretical, with no evidence of actual incidents or near-misses

×

Bias assessment is self-conducted, with no independent verification

×

No escalation path to board, governance sits below SLT level

×

Sub-vendor AI systems not listed, only prime contractor tools disclosed

Part Two

Workforce and People Integrity

The highest-risk blind spot, where AI meets employment law, social value scoring, and human rights

Workforce Integrity

Three Scoring Dimensions Buyers Now Assess

Delivery Workforce

Is AI displacing staff delivering the contract? What is the plan for affected workers? Named MACs under PPN 002 T3.1 and T1.2 are now scored.

PPN 002 · Social Value Model

Recruitment AI

EU AI Act Annex III classes recruitment and workforce AI as high-risk from August 2026. Pre-application disclosure to candidates, a right to human review, and an independent bias audit across all 9 protected characteristics.

EU AI Act Annex III · PSED

Macro Displacement

ONS projects 70% of public sector roles face material AI exposure. Buyers expect a reskilling and redeployment policy, not a promise, but a funded programme with named training lead and outcomes tracking.

ONS 2025 · OECD AI Policy 2024

AI Laundering Warning. Replacing human workers with AI then claiming social value through nominal "reskilling" programmes is now actively screened for. Buyers check whether training is substantive and outcomes-tracked, or reputational window-dressing.

The Standard

Five Workforce Commitments That Exceed Expectations

1

Pre-application disclosure to candidates

All candidates informed which AI tools are used in their selection process, how decisions are made, and how to request human review, before they apply.

2

Independent annual recruitment AI bias audit

Not self-assessed. A named independent auditor, scope covering all nine protected characteristics, outcomes published, findings actioned within 90 days.

3

Funded reskilling programme with named outcomes

A specific budget committed, named training lead, roles at risk identified, quarterly reporting on outcomes against MACs T3.1 baseline.

4

Supply chain AI obligations, contractual not aspirational

All subcontractors and interim staffing agencies bound by the same AI governance standards as the prime contractor. Audit rights included. Breach gives a contractual right.

5

Human override carries no penalty

Staff who override AI recommendations are not penalised in performance reviews. The override is the safeguard. Document the process, train the team, and prove it is used.

Part Three

Service Delivery Integrity

Where governance meets execution, and where Horizon's lesson is most directly applicable

Delivery Standards

Five Standards, From Horizon to Excellence

The Horizon inquiry identified supplier information asymmetry as the root cause. These standards make your governance independently verifiable, not just supplier-certified.

Independent audit rights, no Horizon asymmetry

The buyer has a right to an independent technical audit at any point. No sole reliance on the supplier's own assurance. A named audit scope and frequency are written into the contract.

Red team testing, before and during live service

Adversarial testing against public sector failure scenarios. A named tester, an independent scope, results shared with the buyer, corrective actions tracked.

Live performance dashboard with buyer access

Real-time metrics accessible to the buyer without requesting supplier involvement. Named KPIs, defined thresholds, automatic alerts on deviation.

Output validation process, named methodology

Random sampling of AI outputs by a named human validator. A correction log is maintained. The buyer can request the correction log within 24 hours.

Supply chain AI governance, end to end

Sub-vendor AI systems disclosed, audited annually, and contractually bound. A change notification SLA is included. The buyer must be informed of any material AI system change within 5 working days.

The Horizon principle. "The system never lies." Those words, and the institutional culture they represent, allowed 700+ wrongful convictions. Your governance must be designed so that no one in your organisation, or your client's, can ever operate on that assumption.

Where Suppliers Lose Marks

The Eight Complacency Traps

01 Disclosure gaps

Not naming every AI tool in use, especially embedded third-party or sub-vendor models

02 ATRS obstruction

Claiming commercial sensitivity to avoid publishing Tier 2 algorithmic records

03 Self-certified bias

Bias assessments conducted internally with no independent verification or methodology disclosure

04 Aspirational MACs

Social value commitments with no budget allocation, named lead, or outcome-tracking mechanism

05 Static compliance

A one-time certification with no monitoring process. Governance decays within weeks of contract start

06 Sub-vendor blindness

AI governance applied only to prime contractor systems, with sub-vendor AI disclosed as "out of scope"

07 Human override penalty

Staff have override rights but are performance-managed against AI agreement rates, neutering the safeguard

08 Tender-only artefacts

Governance documents produced for the bid, not maintained operationally. Buyers test this in contract management reviews.

The Excellence Standard

Five Pillars of Supplier Excellence

P1

Proactive Transparency

Disclose before asked. Publish your AI Registry and ATRS records. Name your AI Governance Lead publicly.

P2

Independent Verification

Every material claim independently audited. Bias assessments, technical audits, and red team reports by named third parties.

P3

Workforce Dignity

Candidate disclosure, human override, reskilling with outcomes, supply chain obligations. People are not a compliance box.

P4

Continuous Governance

Governance is live and operational, not tender-produced. Quarterly reviews, named owners, board escalation routes.

P5

Regulatory Agility

A named compliance owner monitoring regulatory change. EU AI Act Aug 2026 plan documented and boarded. ISO 42001 roadmap in place.

The proof portfolio. Nine artefacts separate excellent from compliant. AI Registry, ATRS record, Risk Register, Bias Audit, Data Residency Statement, Human Override Process, Social Value Plan, Supply Chain AI Clause, and Regulatory Monitoring Log.
